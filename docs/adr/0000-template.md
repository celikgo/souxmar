## Context

What is the problem we are deciding about? What constraints apply? What was the trigger for the decision now (a new dependency, a discovered limitation, a roadmap requirement)?

Be specific. Generic context produces generic decisions.

## Decision

The decision in one sentence, then a paragraph of explanation.

State the decision in the active voice: "We use Tauri 2.x as the desktop shell" — not "It was decided that Tauri would probably be used."

## Alternatives considered

At least two alternatives, each with:

- A one-paragraph description.
- Why it was rejected. Be honest — not "Electron is bad," but "Electron's bundle size of 100+ MB is incompatible with our enterprise download-budget constraint."

## Consequences

Three subsections, each a short bulleted list.

### Positive

- What this decision unlocks or simplifies.

### Negative

- What this decision costs us. Honest list. ABI churn cost. Maintenance burden. Skill-set narrowing. Don't pretend a decision has no downsides.

### Risks

- What could go wrong, with a mitigation note.

## Pre-mortem (Tier-3 ADRs only)

It is one year from today, and this decision has gone badly. Write a paragraph describing what happened, in past tense, concretely. Then list the leading indicators we should watch.

## References

- Linked PRs / RFCs / issues.
- Linked external documents.
- Linked code paths once implemented.
